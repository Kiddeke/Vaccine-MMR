Adverse Reactions to MMR Vaccine

Body as a Whole

  • Cardiovascular System Vasculitis: Varies, but fatality rate can range from 5% to 30% or more.
  • Pancreatitis (Digestive System): Can range from 5% to 30% or more.
  • Atypical Measles
  • Panniculitis
  • Diarrhea
  • Vomiting
  • Parotitis

Hematologic and Lymphatic Systems

  • Thrombocytopenia: Approximately 1-2% fatality rate.
  • Purpura: Can range from 1% to 10% fatality rate.
  • Regional Lymphadenopathy: Generally not fatal.
  • Leukocytosis

Immune System

  • Anaphylaxis: Severe allergic reaction.
  • Anaphylactoid Reactions: Non-allergic reactions that resemble anaphylaxis.
  • Angioedema (Including Peripheral or Facial Edema): Swelling of deeper layers of the skin, often in the face or limbs.
  • Bronchial Spasm: Constriction of the airways, often associated with conditions like asthma.
  • Musculoskeletal System: The body's system involving muscles and bones.
  • Arthritis: Inflammation of the joints, causing pain and stiffness.
  • Arthralgia: Joint pain.
  • Myalgia: Muscle pain.

Nervous System

  • Subacute Sclerosing Panencephalitis (SSPE): A progressive neurological disorder caused by the measles virus.
  • Measles Inclusion Body Encephalitis (MIBE): A severe complication of measles infection affecting the brain.
  • Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS): A rare neurological disorder that causes muscle weakness and paralysis.
  • Acute Disseminated Encephalomyelitis (ADEM): An inflammatory disorder of the brain and spinal cord.
  • Encephalitis: Inflammation of the brain.
  • Encephalopathy: A general term for any disease or disorder that affects the brain.
  • Transverse Myelitis: Inflammation of the spinal cord.
  • Ataxia: Lack of muscle coordination.
  • Polyneuritis: Inflammation of multiple nerves.
  • Polyneuropathy: Damage or disease affecting multiple nerves in the body.
  • Ocular Palsies: Weakness or paralysis of eye muscles.
  • Paresthesia: Abnormal sensations like tingling or numbness.
  • Febrile Convulsions: Seizures triggered by fever.
  • Afebrile Convulsions or Seizures: Seizures that occur without fever.
  • Syncope: Fainting or temporary loss of consciousness.

Respiratory System

  • Pneumonia: An inflammatory lung condition that can be severe, especially in certain populations.
  • Stevens-Johnson Syndrome: A severe and potentially life-threatening skin condition.
  • Acute Hemorrhagic Edema of Infancy: A rare but typically self-limiting condition affecting infants.
  • Henoch-Schönlein Purpura: A condition that can cause inflammation of blood vessels and skin rash, often self-limiting.
  • Erythema Multiforme: A skin disorder that can range from mild to more severe forms.
  • Urticaria: Hives, often caused by allergies or infections, usually not life-threatening.
  • Measles-Like Rash: Associated with certain infections, like rubella, typically not life-threatening.
  • Pneumonitis: Inflammation of the lung tissue, which can range from mild to severe.
  • Rash: A skin condition that can vary widely in severity.
  • Pruritus: Itching of the skin, often a symptom of underlying conditions.
  • Sore Throat: Typically not life-threatening.
  • Cough: Typically not life-threatening.
  • Rhinitis: Inflammation of the nasal passages, typically not life-threatening.
  • Injection Site Reactions (Pain, Erythema, Swelling, and Vesiculation): Usually not life-threatening but can vary in severity.

Ear Injuries

  • Ear Nerve Deafness: A severe condition involving the loss of hearing due to nerve damage in the ear.
  • Otitis Media: Inflammation of the middle ear, which can range from mild to more severe but is generally less severe than nerve deafness.

Eye Injuries

  • Eye Retinitis: Inflammation of the retina, which can be a severe condition affecting vision.
  • Optic Neuritis: Inflammation of the optic nerve, potentially leading to vision problems.
  • Papillitis: Inflammation of the optic disc, a part of the optic nerve, which can affect vision.
  • Urogenital System Epididymitis: Inflammation of the epididymis, which can cause pain and discomfort.
  • Orchitis: Inflammation of the testicles, which can be painful and concerning.
  • Conjunctivitis: Inflammation of the conjunctiva, the thin tissue covering the white part of the eye and inner eyelids; typically less severe than the eye and optic nerve conditions listed above.
